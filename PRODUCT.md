# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Adults who want a safe, personal path into movement and better health, especially beginners, older adults, people recovering from injury or surgery, and people who feel uncomfortable in a traditional gym. They are often cautious, evaluating trust and fit before they are ready to make contact.

## Product Purpose

Kristy Fitness introduces Kristy's water training, nutrition coaching, and one-on-one personal training. The site should help a visitor feel understood, see Kristy as a credible and approachable practitioner, and take the low-pressure next step of calling, texting, or scheduling a consultation.

## Brand Personality

Personal, encouraging, and grounded. The voice is warm without becoming sentimental, expert without sounding clinical, and energetic without borrowing the intensity of gym culture.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic fitness templates, aggressive transformation imagery, intimidating gym language, clinical wellness branding, and polished-but-anonymous AI landing-page patterns. The site should not feel like a stack of interchangeable cards, gradients, badges, and icons.

## Design Principles

1. Make Kristy feel present: favor a first-person voice and tangible, human details over generic wellness claims.
2. Lower the barrier to starting: make safety, adaptability, and a no-pressure conversation visible early.
3. Lead with the specialty: give water training a memorable visual and narrative role.
4. Create confidence through clarity: use direct language, readable type, and an obvious path to contact.
5. Show care through craft: every layout, interaction, and detail should feel considered rather than templated.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Keep type comfortably readable for older adults, maintain strong color contrast, provide clear focus states and generous touch targets, respect reduced-motion preferences, and avoid relying on color alone to communicate meaning.
